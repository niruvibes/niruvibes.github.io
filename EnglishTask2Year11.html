    Writing

    The true nature of power, within grand narratives of war, is questioned by prompting new insights from modern-day viewers questioning the truth about the self-centeredness of not only the political elite, but also the everyday man.

    Freud’s analysis of totalitarian political leaders showcases the inherently manipulative, insidious nature of the tyrant motivated by the intoxicating ability of control, attempting to warp the moral sides of humanity into what they deem as perfect.. I picked up this new insight through the poem, “Unknown Citizen”, which represents the story of an individual who has been created as a propagandistic tool to convince others into conforming, becoming the ideal human in the eyes of the government. Throughout the poem we are confronted with the true extent of the locus of power that the government has over the people. The aside “Our report on his Union shows it was sound”, reveals to us how something that is meant to be free of political interference have been meddled with.

    We as readers are offered a view into how the grand narrative of war is manipulated as a technique of storytelling to coerce us as individuals to give into our primal instincts and abandon all the values we keep close as a way of surviving in situations of tyrannical leadership. One such example of this action is in ‘O what is that sound that so thrills the ear’ by WH Auden. Auden utilizes the poetry form as a story telling method, to convey the selfishness of humanity in a dear attempt to survive. In this situation, the man’s desire for survival has led him to abandon his wife on her knees as she attempts to find any form of support, as their house and lives get torn to bits by the impending army sent by the ruler. Auden’s use of the line, “where the vows you swore deceiving, deceiving”, is a connection to the abandonment that she is feeling in her state of fear and panic. Traditional wedding vows express never leaving your partner, “in sickness and in health”, however the husband here has broken the sanctity of his marriage, fear has put him in such a sense of hysteria it has caused him to throw away everything that he loves in a dying attempt at self-preservation. His thought process has been overridden by fear and anxiety and he has become self-interested as his only way of survival. In contradiction to Captain America, in the “grenade scene”, Colonel Phillips utilizes the falling on a grenade test, in an attempt to prove that the soldier “Hodge” deserves the serum as he is seen as the “perfect soldier” as “he's big, he's fast, he obeys orders”. However in attempting to prove Hodge as the better soldier, he reveals that Steve is better as he attempts to cover the grenade with his body, accepting himself as the ultimate sacrifice to save the others,proving how selfless he is, directly opposing the husband from O what is that sound.The same cannot be said for the other soldiers as they attempt to push and shove others as a method of surviving from the grenade, a direct parallel to the husband from O what is that sound. In a typical marriage, the husband is typically seen as the one who protects the family from harm, being the hero figure, however, he has left her on her knees, praying to a higher power in a search for another protective figure as he has left her to prolong the time until his eventual death.
